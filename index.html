FAQ

Q: Why can't I just use ChatGPT?

You can. But then you'll be copy-pasting between browser tabs, babysitting your tone every session, and losing drafts in the abyss of "New Chat." Draftsmith is a blog-post-focused writing cockpit — not a general-purpose AI sandbox.

Q: Will Draftsmith help me grow my audience?

It won’t do your marketing for you, but it makes showing up realistic. Draftsmith gives you a steady flow of publish-ready posts in your own voice, so you can share consistently, build trust, and attract the right readers.

Q: Does it work offline?

Yep. No cloud sync. No SaaS nightmares. (AI generation itself needs internet, of course.)

Q: What's BYOK?

Bring Your Own Key. I don't sell API access—you plug in your OpenAI/Anthropic key and pay them directly.

Q: What if I don't have an API key?

Get one. They're quick to set up and you stay in full control of usage/costs.

Q: What's your refund policy?

Email me within 7 days and I'll refund you — no dramatic breakup speeches required.

Q: Can I use one license on multiple machines?

Nope. One license = one machine. Think of it like a toothbrush — technically you could share it, but you really shouldn't.

Q: Do you store my data?

No. Your drafts live on your machine, not in some mysterious "cloud" server. I don't want your half-finished novel or 3 a.m. rants.

Q: Is there a subscription?

Absolutely not. You pay once, own it forever, and only cry when OpenAI changes their pricing.

Q: Can I import/export my stuff?

Absolutely. Your ideas are yours. Export them, import them, hoard them on a floppy disk if you must — Draftsmith believes in freedom.

Q: What operating systems does Draftsmith work on?

Windows, macOS, and Linux — the holy trinity of desktop computing.

Q: What types of posts can Draftsmith create?

Draftsmith focuses on personal and professional blog posts today — think thought leadership, tutorials, personal stories, and product updates. Roadmap includes emails, tweets, and LinkedIn posts.

Q: Do you send me a CD-ROM?

No. This isn't 1999. The box art is just for nostalgia. You’ll download the app and be typing away faster than you can say “Insert Disc 2.”
