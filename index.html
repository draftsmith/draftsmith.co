FAQ

Q: Why can't I just use ChatGPT?

You can. But then you'll be copy-pasting between browser tabs, babysitting your tone every session, and losing drafts in the abyss of "New Chat." Draftsmith is a blog-post-focused writing cockpit-not a general-purpose AI sandbox.

Q: Does it work offline?

Yep. No cloud sync. No SaaS nightmares.

Q: What's BYOK?

Bring Your Own Key. I don't sell API access-you plug in your OpenAI/Anthropic key and pay them directly.

Q: What if I don't have an API key?

Get one. They're cheap, fast, and won't ask you to name your firstborn.

Q: What's your refund policy?

Email me within 7 days and I'll refund you — no dramatic breakup speeches required.

Q: Why can't I just use ChatGPT (or Claude) for this?

You totally can. If you enjoy juggling prompts, copying and pasting into Google Docs, and losing track of your work in a sea of browser tabs. Draftsmith simply puts all of that in one tidy, offline, no-subscription box - with your writing style baked in.

Q: Can I use one license on multiple machines?

Nope. One license = one machine. Think of it like a toothbrush - technically you could share it, but you really shouldn't.

Q: Does Draftsmith work offline?

Yes... mostly. It's a desktop app, but if you want to summon the AI magic, you'll need the internet. Otherwise, you can stare at your beautiful offline drafts in pure, unplugged bliss.

Q: Do you store my data?

No. Your drafts live on your machine, not in some mysterious "cloud" server. I don't want your half-finished novel or 3 a.m. rants.

Q: Is there a subscription?

Absolutely not. You pay once, own it forever, and only cry when OpenAI changes their pricing.

Q: Can I import/export my stuff?

Absolutely. Your ideas are yours. Export them, import them, hoard them on a floppy disk if you must — Draftsmith believes in freedom.

Q: Will Draftsmith make me a better writer?

Only emotionally. The words will be there; the therapy bills are still on you.

Q: What operating systems does Draftsmith work on?

Windows, macOS, and Linux - the holy trinity of desktop computing. If you're still on Windows XP or running something exotic like "BeOS," Draftsmith will politely decline to install.

Q: What about bugs?

Oh, they exist. I call them "quirks." If you find one, report it - I might fix it, or I might write a heartfelt blog post about it instead.

Q: What types of posts can Draftsmith create?

Draftsmith focuses on personal and professional blog posts today — not SEO spam or automated LinkedIn threads. Think thought leadership, tutorials, personal stories, and company updates. Future roadmap includes emails, tweets, and LinkedIn posts.

Q: Do you send me a CD-ROM?

No. This isn't 1999, and I'm not AOL. The box art is just for nostalgia. You'll download the app and be typing away faster than you can say "Insert Disc 2."
